# Audire: AI and the Arts Reflection

## Design Position

Audire treats AI as a reflective music-learning partner, not an authority on taste. The product is designed around a tension central to AI in the arts: computational suggestions can improve access to explanation and experimentation, but they can also standardize creative choices or hide uncertainty behind confident language.

## Three Implemented Features

### 1. Creative Paths

Each analysis offers three directions:

- **Preserve** makes the smallest useful change.
- **Develop** moves toward the musician's stated style and goal.
- **Explore** offers a defensible but bolder alternative.

Every path includes a progression, musical rationale, and artistic tradeoff. This prevents the AI from presenting one answer as the correct future of the musician's idea.

### 2. Listening Lab

The musician can hear the original progression beside each AI suggestion. Playback makes the musician's ear part of evaluation instead of asking them to trust text alone. The AI can explain a progression, but the musician decides whether it works in practice.

### 3. Practice and Agency Lab

Audire turns analysis into a measurable three-step practice session with a metronome and completion tracking. The same workspace exposes:

- Evidence directly supported by the input
- Assumptions used by the model
- Musical information the model still does not know
- Confidence in the harmonic interpretation
- A personal-style rating and reflection note

This design makes uncertainty visible and gives the final creative judgment back to the musician.

## Course Themes

### Creativity and Authorship

Audire generates possibilities, but the musician selects, changes, or rejects them. Authorship remains with the person who makes the musical decision.

### Agency

The interface avoids a single optimized answer. Choosing among Preserve, Develop, and Explore makes agency an interaction rather than a disclaimer.

### Accessibility

Structured explanations, playable examples, and guided practice make music-theory feedback more accessible to self-taught musicians who may not have regular access to lessons.

### Standardization

AI models may default to familiar genre patterns. Audire counters that pressure by showing tradeoffs, preserving an original path, and asking whether a suggestion still sounds personal.

### Limits of Text-Based AI

Chord symbols do not reveal voicing, rhythm, melody, touch, dynamics, or intention. Audire reports those unknowns rather than pretending it heard a performance. Future audio or MIDI analysis would add context, but it should not remove the musician's role as the final listener.
