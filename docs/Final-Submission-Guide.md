# Final Submission Guide

## Recommended Package

1. A 4–6 minute screen-recorded demonstration.
2. The live Audire link, with the video as a fallback.
3. `Final-Critical-Reflection.md` exported to the format requested by the instructor.
4. A short process note or the final minute of the video explaining the first incorrect ii-V result and the redesign it caused.

## Demo Script

### 0:00–0:35 — Purpose

Introduce Audire as an AI-assisted learning tool for self-taught musicians. State that it supports reflection and practice rather than generating a finished song or deciding what is musically correct.

### 0:35–1:20 — Ambiguity

Enter `Dm7 - Gm7` without a tonal centre. Show that Audire does not force one harmonic interpretation and exposes what it cannot know from text, including voicing, rhythm, touch, and intended tonic.

### 1:20–2:10 — Contextual Analysis

Set the tonal centre to `D minor`, style to `R&B`, and goal to `I need passing chords`. Analyze again. Point out the corrected i7–iv7 reading and the concrete note movement in the feedback.

### 2:10–3:15 — Creative Agency

Compare Preserve, Develop, and Explore. Read one tradeoff aloud. Use the Listening Lab to play the original and two alternatives, then explain that the musician chooses by ear rather than accepting a single ranked answer.

### 3:15–4:05 — Learning Workflow

Show the three-step practice plan, tempo control, metronome, completion tracking, and reflection note. Emphasize that the output becomes something a musician can practise, not merely a paragraph to read.

### 4:05–5:00 — Critical Reflection

Open the AI Lens. Connect evidence and unknowns to Bell’s critique of hidden “magic.” Connect multiple paths and tradeoffs to Brook’s concern with standardization. Mention cultural bias and the risk of reducing gospel, jazz, or R&B to familiar chord clichés.

### 5:00–5:30 — Process and Limits

Explain that an early model response incorrectly called `Dm7 - Gm7` a ii-V. Show how that failure led to key input, stricter instructions, uncertainty reporting, and the music-theory evaluation suite. Close by stating that authorship remains with the musician.

## Recording Checklist

- Use a fresh browser window and 100% zoom.
- Confirm the live backend before recording.
- Keep one tested progression ready in case the API is temporarily slow.
- Record system audio so chord playback and the metronome are audible.
- Avoid showing environment files, API keys, dashboards, or terminal history.
- Submit the video even when the live link works; it preserves the intended demonstration.
